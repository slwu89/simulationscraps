\hypertarget{discrete-time-petri-nets-molloy-1985}{%
\subsection{Discrete Time Petri Nets (Molloy
1985)}\label{discrete-time-petri-nets-molloy-1985}}

Molloy's equation 4 is
\[ \rho_{i} = \frac{P[E_{i}]}{P{E_[{i}]}\cup P[E_{0}]} \]. The
probability \(\rho_{i}\) are assigned by the designer \textbf{a priori},
the RHS of the expression is the definition, ``the probability that the
enabled transition \(t_{i}\) fires at the next time step, given
(conditioned on) the fact that no other transition fires.'' The
conditioning gives the denominator. So it's made up of more fundamental
building blocks that the conflict resolution rule in the Petri Net
simulator has to deal with.

In a set of mutually exclusive transitions \(\{t_{i}\}\), that equation
holds for all, so using the constraint all \(0<\rho_{i}<1\), one can
solve for \(P[E_{0}]\) (he does not show this).
